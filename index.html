Frequently Asked Questions

  • How to add support?

    • The model’s volume is related to its weight. The general model has 4-5 sizes of supports, which are divided into S, M, L, XL and other models (S<M<L<XL). Reference standard: S for less than 1000 volume units, M for 1000-10000, L for 10000-40000, and XL for above 40,000. Please fully understand the model structure before adding supports. Adjust the supports with different thickness according to the condition of the model, so that it can be detached and finished details will not be affected.

  • How to select slicing parameters?

    • The selection of slicing parameters is related to the resin used. Different resins are for different slicing parameters. Generally, there is a resin option behind the material. After selecting the resin type, the corresponding slicing parameters can be selected.

  • How to do post-processing?

    • Tools used for model’s post-processing include needle-nosed pliers, knife, spatula, brushes, alcohol, and polishing rod. After the model is printed, clean it with alcohol, blow it dry with a hair dryer (uses cold air), cut off the supports with a needle-nosed pliers along the support head, and cut off the residual support head with a knife. Polish the unevenness after supports are removed. At this time, there will be a lot of residue on the model, brush the residue with alcohol.

  • How to check and repair the model data?

    • If the model has problems such as unclosed gaps, bad edges, and reverse triangle patches, the problem area will display anomalies or failure to add support when import data into slicing software, which will directly cause printing failure or model’s missing. Therefore, it is necessary to use the third-party repair software Magics to check the model data before pringting to avoid the above problems.

       

  • What are the requirements for the model format?

    • Our slicing software supports files in stl and obj format. Currently, other formats are not supported, but the format can be converted to stl or obj by 3DMax, solidworks, CAD and other softwares.

       

  • How to store the resin?

    • Photosensitive resin needs to be stored away from light, and can be stored in a dark container at room temperature and humidity. After storage for a long time, the resin needs to be stirred before using for printing. The resin’s shelf life is generally 1 year. Pay attention to the storage time to avoid expiration.
